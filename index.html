BA-PHERD: Big Data Analytics Pipeline for the Identification of Heterogeneous Extracellular non-coding RNAs as Disease Biomarkers

The study of functional associations between biological entities and human diseases is a pivotal task of modern research, with the ultimate goal of developing more effective and personalized therapeutic approaches. Among the most promising avenues in this direction is the analysis of extracellular RNAs (exRNAs), RNA molecules detectable outside the cell in biofluids such as blood, urine, and saliva, whose profiles have been reported as diagnostic, prognostic, and theranostic biomarkers for a wide range of diseases, including cancer, Alzheimer's, and Parkinson's disease.

Current computational approaches for exRNA biomarker discovery, however, face severe limitations: existing machine learning methods struggle to jointly handle the heterogeneity of biological entities (miRNAs, lncRNAs, circRNAs), the massive scale and multi-view nature of the data, and the sensitivity of clinical sources, all of which are simultaneously present in exRNA analysis. Moreover, data governance solutions for biological pipelines remain coarse-grained, platform-dependent, and ill-suited to dynamic coalitions of institutions sharing sensitive data.

To overcome these challenges, BA-PHERD defines a complete Big Data Analytics pipeline, from data acquisition and ingestion to model learning, evaluation, and sharing, purpose-built for heterogeneous exRNA analysis.

BA-PHERD relies on 3 pillars:

  • novel machine learning methods for heterogeneous attributed network data, supporting node classification, link prediction, and subgraph identification without relying on manually defined meta-paths;
  • a tailored data governance solution based on an enhanced, annotation-driven access control system that balances data sharing within research coalitions with strict data protection requirements;
  • biological validation, combining bioinformatics analysis and a pilot experimental study on neurodegenerative disease biomarkers (Alzheimer's, Parkinson's, Lewy Body Dementia) using patient-derived biofluid samples.

The synergic exploitation of these pillars, grounded in Graph Neural Networks and Relational Learning and validated in a real clinical scenario, makes BA-PHERD a holistic framework pursuing an innovative, data-sovereign approach to biomarker discovery. BA-PHERD represents a decisive step toward non-invasive, cost-effective early diagnosis of human diseases, with direct impact on patients, healthcare systems, and the broader biomedical research community.
